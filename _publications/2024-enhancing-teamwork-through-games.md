This systematic literature review examines the role of games in enhancing teamwork across educational and professional settings. The study explores how game-based learning environments foster collaboration, communication, problem-solving, and group coordination. Drawing on peer-reviewed literature published after 2010, the review synthesizes evidence from studies retrieved from Taylor & Francis, PubMed, and IEEE Xplore databases.

The findings highlight the effectiveness of games as instructional tools for developing teamwork competencies and supporting collaborative learning experiences. The review also discusses challenges associated with teaching teamwork and demonstrates how game-based approaches can create engaging environments that strengthen interpersonal skills, collective decision-making, and team performance.
