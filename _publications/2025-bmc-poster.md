This research investigates the application of machine learning techniques for cardiovascular disease risk prediction using large-scale clinical datasets. Logistic regression and decision tree models were employed to identify key predictors of cardiovascular disease and evaluate their relative importance. Results demonstrated that Chest Pain Type and ST Depression were among the most influential predictors, while logistic regression achieved superior predictive performance compared to decision trees. The findings highlight the potential of data-driven approaches to support clinical decision-making, improve risk assessment, and inform public health interventions for cardiovascular disease management.
