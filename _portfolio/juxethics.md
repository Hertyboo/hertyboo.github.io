## Overview

**JuxEthics** is a web-based AI ethics readiness assessment tool designed to evaluate how organizations communicate their commitments to responsible AI. The platform analyzes publicly available corporate disclosures, policies, reports, and governance statements to identify evidence of AI governance practices related to transparency, accountability, fairness, privacy, safety, human oversight, and human-centered design. Using these signals, JuxEthics generates an ethics readiness profile that helps organizations, researchers, policymakers, and other stakeholders better understand organizational preparedness for responsible AI adoption and governance.

## My Role as a Co-Founder

As co-founder, I contributed to the conceptualization and development of the assessment framework, research design, governance indicators, and ethical evaluation criteria. My work focuses on translating complex principles of AI ethics and governance into practical assessment mechanisms that can support organizational self-assessment and responsible AI decision-making.

## Research Significance
As AI technologies become increasingly integrated into organizational decision-making, stakeholders face growing challenges in assessing whether organizations are prepared to develop, deploy, and govern AI responsibly. JuxEthics addresses this challenge by exploring how public disclosures can be transformed into structured indicators of AI ethics readiness. The project contributes to ongoing conversations about AI governance, accountability, transparency, and responsible innovation by providing a framework for evaluating organizational commitments to ethical AI practices.

## Connection to Current Research
JuxEthics directly supports my broader research agenda in AI Ethics and Governance, Human-Centered AI, and Responsible AI. The project investigates how organizations communicate ethical commitments, how governance principles can be operationalized into measurable indicators, and how assessment tools can support transparency and accountability in AI adoption. More broadly, the project reflects my interest in developing practical governance mechanisms that help organizations implement AI systems in ways that promote fairness, accessibility, human oversight, and societal trust.
