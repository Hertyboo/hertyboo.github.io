--- layout: archive title: "Teaching" permalink: /teaching/ author_profile: true --- {% include base_path %} My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that meaningful learning occurs when students feel supported, challenged, and empowered to connect course concepts to real-world problems. I strive to create inclusive, accessible, and student-centered learning environments that recognize the diverse experiences, backgrounds, and learning needs students bring to the classroom. Drawing on principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and evidence-based pedagogy, I design learning experiences that promote engagement, critical thinking, collaboration, and practical application. As Instructor of Record for CNIT 13600 at Purdue University, I have applied these principles while teaching more than 1,000 undergraduate students in large-enrollment online courses. To support diverse learners, I developed detailed course guides, technology support resources for both Windows and Mac users, flexible communication channels through virtual office hours and Microsoft Teams, and structured weekly learning modules that enabled students to navigate course content independently. My research in AI Ethics and Governance, Human-Centered AI, and Educational Technology further informs my teaching by encouraging students to critically examine the societal implications of technology while developing practical technical skills. Through inclusive course design, accessible learning practices, and student-centered instruction, I seek to foster equitable participation, support student success, and prepare learners to become thoughtful professionals, responsible technology users, and lifelong learners. ## Courses ## CNIT 13600: Personal Computing Technology and Applications CNIT 13600 is a foundational undergraduate computing course that introduces students to personal computing technologies, digital literacy, information systems, and productivity applications. The course combines conceptual understanding of information technology with hands-on experience using Microsoft Office applications, including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Access. Students also explore topics such as digital ethics, privacy, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and responsible technology use. As Instructor of Record, I have taught more than 1000 undergraduate students across multiple semesters in large-enrollment online sections ranging from approximately 250 to 370 students.
