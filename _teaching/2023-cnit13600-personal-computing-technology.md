CNIT 13600 is a foundational undergraduate computing course that introduces students to personal computing technologies, digital literacy, information systems, and productivity applications. The course combines conceptual understanding of information technology with hands-on experience using Microsoft Office applications, including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Access. Students also explore topics such as digital ethics, privacy, cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and responsible technology use.
As Instructor of Record, I have taught more than 1000 undergraduate students across multiple semesters in large-enrollment online sections ranging from approximately 250 to 370 students. The course serves students from a wide range of disciplines and academic backgrounds, requiring instructional approaches that support varying levels of technical experience and digital literacy.
