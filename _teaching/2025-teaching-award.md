Awarded Purdue University's highest university-level teaching honor for graduate students through a highly competitive, campus-wide selection process recognizing excellence in teaching, instructional leadership, student learning, and contributions to the educational mission of the university. Recipients are selected from a pool of nominees identified as the strongest teaching candidates across Purdue's colleges and schools and are evaluated through a rigorous review of teaching effectiveness, course design, mentoring, student feedback, and documented achievements in teaching and learning.
In the award notification, the selection committee specifically recognized the “systematic application of my teaching philosophy” and “systematic engagement in the scholarship of teaching and learning, especially on the ethics of AI use.” The award reflects my commitment to evidence-based, inclusive, and student-centered teaching practices that promote accessibility, engagement, and equitable participation in learning.
As Instructor of Record for CNIT 13600, I have taught more than 1000 undergraduate students in large-enrollment online courses while maintaining consistently strong student evaluations. Across multiple semesters, students rated my teaching highly for communication (4.30–4.43/5), fairness in assessment (4.33–4.46/5), and creation of an inclusive learning environment (4.23–4.43/5), with response rates ranging from 79% to 83%. My teaching integrates principles of Universal Design for Learning (UDL), accessibility, and responsible technology use to support diverse learners and foster meaningful student success.
