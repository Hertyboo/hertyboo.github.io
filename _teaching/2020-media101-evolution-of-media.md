MEDIA 101 explored the historical development of communication technologies and media systems, examining how technological, social, cultural, economic, and organizational forces have shaped the evolution of media and communication industries. As a Graduate Teaching Assistant, I supported course instruction through grading assignments, providing individualized and formative feedback, monitoring student performance, and assisting faculty in identifying students who could benefit from additional academic support and intervention. Through the analysis of student learning patterns, engagement data, and performance trends on Brightspace, I contributed to efforts aimed at improving student engagement, academic success, and retention.
These experiences played a significant role in my development as both an educator and researcher. Working closely with student learning data deepened my understanding of how learners interact with educational technologies and highlighted the importance of timely feedback, targeted interventions, and learner-centered instructional design. Observing how technology influences communication, learning, and participation sparked my interest in the broader relationship between technology and human behavior. These experiences ultimately shaped my current research interests in Human-Centered AI and Educational Technology, particularly the design, evaluation, and governance of equitable, accessible, and user-centered technologies that support learning, engagement, and student success.
